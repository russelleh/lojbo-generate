Lojban is a constructed language that avoids ambiguity and reflects the principles of logic.

Lojban is lexically unambiguous, meaning that each word has a unique definition and a unique spelling.

pritu right-hand side

drani correct

kurjga square angle

Lojban letters correspond to sounds exactly, meaning that each word is spoken in a unique way.

cinmo emotion

cutci shoe

karce car

kurfa square

fonxa phone

rufsu rough

xedja jaw

mutce much

Lojban uses words instead of special characters for punctuation and emoticons.

xu do taske Are you thirsty?

.ui xamgu Good :)

la .alis. bacru lu coi li'u Alice said "hello"

It segments words based on emphasis, meaning no two distinct sentences are spoken the same.

basna da emphasize something

ba snada succeed

Lojban is still semantically ambiguous, meaning that words with the same definition may refer to different things. Pronouns have no case, animacy, or gender.

mi I

mi me

mi us

ta him

ta her

ta it

It has no default number or tense.

xrula a flower

xrula flowers

Lojban sentences are syntactically unambiguous, meaning that grammar may not have multiple valid interpretations.

mi na'e dansu ki'u lo zgike I did not dance, because of the music

mi dansu ki'u nai lo zgike I danced, not because of the music

The grammar is based on concepts from formal logic.

la .alis. .a la .bab. stali Alice and/or Bob stay

la .alis. .e la .bab. stali Alice and Bob stay

la .alis. .o la .bab. stali Alice stays if and only if Bob does

la .alis. .u la .bab. stali Alice stays whether or not Bob does

la .alis. .o nai la .bab. stali Alice or Bob stay

la .alis. na .a la .bab. stali Alice stays only if Bob does

Instead of prepositions, word order determines the meaning of a sentence.

mi skicu lo tcika do I tell the time of day to you

do skicu lo nuzba mi You tell the news to me
